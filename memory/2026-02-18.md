Today's session focused on troubleshooting the video playback issue in the HBS tutor app on iOS. Key events and takeaways:

- The build process for the app was initially failing due to symlink and large asset issues, including referencing files outside the app bundle, which is not allowed by iOS.
- The symlink referencing large_assets/hbsvideo was removed, and the Xcode project was reverted to an earlier working version to resolve issues.
- The app build completed successfully, but video playback failed, logging the attempted path: `./hbsvideo/Module_7/7.4.1%20Time%20Value%20of%20Money%20-%20Mozilla%20Firefox%202020-08-04%2021-13-24.mp4`.
- Despite the corrected build, videos could not be found during playback because the required `hbsvideo` directory and its contents were not fully accessible to the app at runtime.

Next steps:
- Investigate whether the `hbsvideo` directory and videos are correctly copied into the app bundle during the build process.
- Confirm that the `player.html` script has the correct paths and fallback mechanisms for locating video files.
- Ensure the videos are either included in the app bundle or accessible via an external resource suitable for iOS sandboxing.
