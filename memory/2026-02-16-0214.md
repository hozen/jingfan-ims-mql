## What "Value Capture" Really Means

True "value capture" in bundling economics refers to extracting more value from customers by leveraging differences in their willingness to pay for individual components. For example:

- Customer A values hardware at $60,000 and software at $5,000
- Customer B values hardware at $40,000 and software at $15,000
- Without bundling, you might sell hardware to A for $60,000 and software to B for $15,000
- With bundling, you might get both customers to pay $54,000 each for the bundle
- The "value capture" is the additional revenue from customer A (who would have paid $65,000 total but pays $54,000) and the fact that customer B now purchases the bundle instead of just the software
